STATISTICS OF CYBERSECURITY AWARENESS IN MALAYSIA

  • Age Group
  • Gender
  • Education Level
  • Time spent on internet

Awareness Rate by Age Group

There's a noticeable disparity in cyber security awareness across different age groups. The younger age group (19 - 25) demonstrates the highest awareness rate, which might be attributed to their higher exposure to technology and digital environments from an early age. On the other hand, the older age groups (26 - 35 and Over 46) exhibit relatively lower awareness rates, indicating a potential need for targeted cyber security education efforts for these demographics.

Awareness Rate by Gender

The data indicates a significant difference in cyber security awareness between genders, with males showing a higher awareness rate compared to females. This could be due to various factors such as differences in exposure to cyber security education, experience with technology, or access to resources and information related to cyber threats.

Awareness Rate by Education Level

Individuals with a Diploma/ A Level/ Matrikulasi/ STPM have the highest awareness rate (61.5%), followed by those with a Degree (23.1%) and individuals with a Master's degree (15.4%). This indicates that higher levels of education correlate with a higher awareness of cybersecurity issues, possibly due to exposure to related topics in academic settings.

Awareness Rate by Time Spent on the Internet

Individuals who spend above 6 hours on the internet per day have the highest awareness rate (61.5%), followed by those who spend 2 - 4 hours (19.2%) and 4 - 6 hours (11.5%). Those who spend below 2 hours have the lowest awareness rate (7.7%). This suggests that increased internet usage correlates with higher awareness of cybersecurity issues, possibly due to greater exposure to online risks.

Awareness Rate by State of Residence

The data shows variations in cyber security awareness among different states of residence. Kuala Lumpur has the highest awareness rate, possibly due to its status as the capital city and its more urbanized population, which may have better access to cyber security education and resources. Pahang and Terengganu exhibit similar awareness rates, suggesting a need for enhanced cyber security awareness initiatives in these states.

Cyber safety tips

PROTECT yourself against cyberattacks

1. Update your software and operating system: This means you benefit from the latest security patches.

2. Use anti-virus software: Security solutions like Kaspersky Total Security will detect and removes threats. Keep your software updated for the best level of protection.

3. Use strong passwords: Ensure your passwords are not easily guessable.

4. Do not open email attachments from unknown senders: These could be infected with malware.
